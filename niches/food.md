# Food Creator Playbook

Applies when the creator's niche is food, cooking, restaurant reviews, or
culinary content.

## What to monitor for this niche

Season (what's fresh/in-season), local food festivals, street food scenes,
trending cafes/restaurants in the creator's city, regional specialties,
and food-related cultural events/holidays.

## Content angles that work for this niche

- **Taste test / review** — genuine reaction matters more than polish;
  audiences trust visible authentic reaction over scripted praise
- **Street food tour** — works especially well combined with "hidden
  places locals actually go" framing rather than obvious tourist spots
- **Hidden places** — specificity (exact stall/dish, not just "great
  food here") is what makes this save-worthy for someone planning a visit
- **Seasonal cooking** — tying a recipe/dish to what's actually in season
  right now feels timely and gives a natural reason to post it today
  rather than any other day
- **Community questions** — "what should I try next" or "settle this
  debate" formats drive strong comment engagement in this niche

## Format notes

- Close-up, well-lit shots of the food itself are non-negotiable — this
  is a highly visual niche where the thumbnail/first frame decides
  whether someone stops scrolling
- Reels showing the eating moment (first bite reaction) tend to
  outperform reels that only show preparation
- Carousels work well for "ranked list" or "X places to try" formats

## Guardrail note

Never present a paid promotion (restaurant comping a meal, brand
sponsorship) as an unbiased organic recommendation without disclosure —
this is both an ethics issue and often a legal requirement depending on
the creator's location.
