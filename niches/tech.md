# Tech Creator Playbook

Applies when the creator's niche is tech, AI, programming, or developer
tools.

## What to monitor for this niche

AI models/LLMs, open source releases, GitHub trending repos, developer
tools, major company announcements (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta,
Microsoft, Apple, NVIDIA), startup funding news, security/vulnerability
news, programming language trends, cloud platform updates.

If tool access is available, check these sources before generating ideas.
If not, ask the creator what's caught their attention in tech recently.

## Content angles that work for this niche

- **Breaking news reel** — fast reaction within hours of a launch beats a
  polished take days later; speed matters more than production value here
- **Comparison** — new tool vs. the incumbent it's competing with, tested
  hands-on rather than just reading the announcement
- **Tutorial** — practical "how to actually use this" beats theoretical
  explanation; developers save tutorials, they don't just watch them
- **First impressions / hands-on** — genuine reaction, including
  limitations found, builds more trust than pure hype
- **Hidden features** — deep-dive on something the mainstream coverage
  missed; rewards creators who actually use the tool, not just read docs
- **Mistakes/gotchas** — "the bug that cost me 3 hours" format performs
  well because it's specific, relatable, and save-worthy
- **Future predictions** — riskier format, works best from creators with
  established credibility in the space

## Format notes

- Code-on-screen content needs large, readable text — assume viewers are
  on mobile, zoom in or simplify snippets rather than showing a full IDE
- Carousels work well for step-by-step tutorials; Reels work better for
  reactions/comparisons
- This audience saves and shares technical reference content heavily —
  optimize captions with the exact terms people would search

## Guardrail note

Never claim hands-on testing that didn't happen, or present speculation
as confirmed fact about unreleased products — tech audiences are quick to
call this out and it damages credibility fast.
