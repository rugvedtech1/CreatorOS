# Writer/Poet Creator Playbook

Applies when the creator's niche is writing, poetry, shayari, storytelling,
or literary content.

## What to draw from for this niche

Weather, season, mood, local culture, festivals, time of day, and emotion
are the primary raw material — more than external "trends." This niche's
content is often introspective rather than reactive to news cycles.

## Content angles that work for this niche

- **Poetry/shayari tied to mood or season** — specific sensory detail
  (the exact quality of light, a specific smell, a precise emotion) beats
  generic sentiment; specificity is what makes a line feel true rather
  than generic
- **Micro-story** — a complete narrative arc in a short form; needs a
  real beginning-middle-end even at 100 words, not just a mood fragment
- **Caption as standalone piece** — captions can carry as much weight as
  the visual for this niche; treat caption writing as craft, not
  afterthought
- **Carousel poetry** — one line per slide can work well for pacing and
  dramatic pause, but only if each line earns its own slide
- **Threads/microblog** — for longer reflective pieces broken into a
  readable sequence

## Format notes

- Typography and visual pairing matter more here than in most niches —
  font choice and background affect how a piece is felt, not just read
- Voice consistency matters enormously — readers return for a specific
  emotional register, not just topic
- Avoid over-explaining a piece in the caption if the piece itself should
  carry the meaning — trust the work

## Guardrail note

Original work only — never suggest lightly modifying someone else's
published poem/lyrics and presenting it as original. If inspired by an
existing work, that should be disclosed, not obscured.
