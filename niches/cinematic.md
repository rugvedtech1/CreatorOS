# Cinematic Creator Playbook

Applies when the creator's niche is cinematic video content, filmmaking,
or video essay style work.

## What to monitor for this niche

Film/movie releases (for reaction/analysis content), trending editing
styles or transitions, and music/audio trends suited to cinematic pacing.

## Content angles that work for this niche

- **Shot list planning** — helping the creator plan B-roll and hero shots
  in advance produces more cohesive content than improvised shooting
- **B-roll suggestions** — specific to their subject/theme, not generic
  stock-footage-style suggestions
- **Drone shots** — where legally/practically viable, adds production
  value; note when it's not necessary just for its own sake
- **Transitions** — should serve the story/pacing, not be used purely for
  novelty; overused flashy transitions can undercut a cinematic feel
- **Camera movement** — matching movement style (static, handheld, glide)
  to the emotional tone of the piece
- **Music mood matching** — music should set pacing and emotional
  register before any cut is made, not be added as an afterthought
- **Color grading / LUT choice** — consistent grading across a creator's
  work builds visual brand identity (ties to `engines/personal-branding.md`)
- **Voiceover** — should add meaning/context the visuals alone can't
  carry, not narrate what's already visually obvious

## Format notes

- This niche typically favors longer-form Reels/videos over quick
  Stories — production value and pacing matter more than posting
  frequency
- Sound design (ambient audio, not just music) often separates amateur
  from polished cinematic content — worth flagging as a differentiator

## Guardrail note

Using copyrighted music/footage without rights (even for "educational"
framing) risks takedown and demonetization — recommend licensed/royalty-
free sources.
