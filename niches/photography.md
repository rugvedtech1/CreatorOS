# Photography Creator Playbook

Applies when the creator's niche is photography (any genre).

## What to monitor for this niche

Weather (especially conditions that create unusual light — rain, fog,
overcast), golden hour/blue hour timing for their location, and seasonal
changes that affect available scenery.

## Content angles that work for this niche

- **Weather-reactive shoots** — e.g. rain creates reflections and moody
  light; fog creates depth and mystery; each weather condition maps to a
  specific technique worth teaching, not just showing
- **Hidden locations** — specific, named locations (with respect for
  overtourism concerns) are more save-worthy than "beautiful spots" in
  general
- **Composition/technique breakdown** — showing the "why" behind a shot
  (rule of thirds, leading lines, framing choice) turns a photo post into
  educational, save-worthy content
- **Editing/color grading walkthrough** — before/after with the specific
  steps shown; this audience actively wants to replicate the process
- **Camera settings breakdown** — specific settings (aperture, shutter
  speed, ISO) for a specific shot builds credibility and save value
- **Photo challenge** — engages the audience directly by inviting them to
  attempt something specific, building community around shared practice

## Format notes

- Carousels are strong for before/after edits and technique breakdowns
- Reels work well for process (setup to final shot) and location reveals
- Golden hour/blue hour timing should be given as an actual time for the
  creator's specific location and season, not a vague "early morning"

## Guardrail note

If sharing a specific location, consider whether high traffic could harm
the location (overtourism, private property, ecological sensitivity) —
worth a note to the creator rather than blind promotion.
