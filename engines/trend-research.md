# Trend Research Engine

Use this file when the creator asks "what's trending," wants trend-based
content ideas, or when the daily dashboard needs current trend context.

## Step 1 — Research honestly, using real tools when available

Same rule as daily-intelligence.md: never invent trends. If this
environment has live search/tool access, use it to check relevant
platforms and sources for the creator's niche:

- Instagram (Explore/Reels trends, trending audio in their niche)
- YouTube (what's gaining traction in adjacent content)
- Reddit / X (conversation happening in their space)
- Google Trends (search interest patterns)
- GitHub trending (tech creators specifically)
- Niche-specific news sources (industry sites for their vertical)

If no tool access is available, ask the creator what they've been seeing
trend in their feed/niche recently, and work from that.

## Step 2 — Filter for relevance, not just popularity

A trend being popular doesn't mean it fits this creator. Before suggesting
it, check:

- Does it fit their niche naturally, or would it feel forced/off-brand?
- Can they add genuine original value, or would this just be a copy?
- Is it early enough to still get reach, or already saturated/declining?
- Does it conflict with their brand values or audience expectations?

If a trend fails these checks, don't force it just because it's popular —
suggest the creator skip it and note why.

## Step 3 — The "original value" requirement

This is the core discipline of this engine: trends are inspiration, never
a template to copy verbatim. For every trend suggested, also specify what
makes THIS creator's take different:

- Their specific expertise/perspective applied to the trend format
- A niche-specific angle other creators in the trend haven't covered
- Their own voice/humor/presentation style layered on top

Bad trend usage: reusing someone else's exact format/audio/joke with
nothing added. Good trend usage: using a trending format as a container,
filled with the creator's own original insight.

## Step 4 — Trend lifecycle awareness

Help the creator understand timing, since this affects urgency:

- **Early stage**: low competition, high reward if it fits, but riskier
  since it might not take off — good for creators who post frequently
  and can afford to experiment
- **Peak stage**: high visibility but high competition — needs a genuinely
  strong original angle to stand out, not just participation
- **Declining stage**: generally skip, unless creator has a specific
  reason (like commentary on the trend itself)

## Step 5 — Output format

When presenting trend research, give:
1. The trend itself and where it's currently happening
2. Why it might fit (or not fit) this creator specifically
3. The original angle to apply if they use it
4. Urgency read (early/peak/declining)

## Guardrail

Trend-jacking should never cross into misleading framing (e.g., using a
trending audio/format to bait engagement unrelated to the actual content).
Cross-check against `ethics/safety-guardrails.md`.
