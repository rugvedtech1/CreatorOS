# Personal Branding Engine

Use this file when the creator asks about voice, positioning, visual
identity, or "how do I stand out" / "what's my brand."

## The five pillars

1. **Trust** — built through consistency between what the creator says and
   does, and through admitting mistakes/limitations rather than projecting
   false authority. Trust compounds slowly and breaks quickly.

2. **Authority** — demonstrated through specific, verifiable expertise
   (results, process transparency, depth on their actual topic) rather
   than claimed through titles or hype. Show the work, don't just state
   the credential.

3. **Voice** — the consistent way the creator sounds: word choice, humor
   style, pacing, what they're willing to say that others in the niche
   avoid. Voice is what makes content recognizable without seeing the
   creator's face or name.

4. **Visual identity** — consistent color grading, font choice, thumbnail
   style, or framing style that makes content recognizable while
   scrolling, before the viewer even reads anything.

5. **Positioning** — the specific angle that separates this creator from
   others in the same niche. "Photography tips" is not a position;
   "photography for people who can't afford good gear" is.

## Finding positioning (ask these questions)

- What does this creator believe about their niche that others don't say
  out loud?
- What's a common frustration in their niche that's underserved?
- What's the intersection of their niche + a second interest/skill that
  few competitors combine?
- Who do they NOT want to be compared to, and why — this often reveals
  the actual differentiation instinctively.

## Consistency checklist

When reviewing a creator's existing content for brand consistency, check:
- Does the visual style stay recognizable across different post types?
- Does the voice/tone stay consistent, or does it swing depending on
  mood/trend of the day?
- Do the topics covered stay within a coherent identity, or feel
  scattered without a throughline?

Note: consistency doesn't mean rigid sameness — it means a recognizable
core that flexes, not a formula that never changes.

## Brand values

Help the creator articulate 2-4 values that guide content decisions (e.g.,
"always show real results, never staged," "humor over lecturing"). These
act as a filter for future content and collab decisions — when unsure
whether to take an opportunity, check it against these values first.

## Guardrail

Positioning should never be built on fabricated credentials, exaggerated
results, or a persona that misrepresents who the creator actually is —
this collapses trust the moment it's discovered. See
`ethics/safety-guardrails.md`.
