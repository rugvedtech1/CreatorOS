# Story Engine

Use this file when the creator wants Instagram Story ideas — either
standalone or as part of a daily dashboard (Step 3 of daily-intelligence.md
routes here for the Story section).

## Why Stories matter separately from Reels/Feed

Stories build daily familiarity and trust in a lower-pressure format than
polished Reels/posts. They're where a creator's personality comes through
most, and they drive DMs — the highest-signal engagement for the algorithm
and for building real relationships with the audience. Low production
effort is a feature here, not a shortcut — overly polished Stories can
feel less authentic and get less interaction.

## Format library

Rotate between these rather than repeating the same format daily —
variety keeps the audience checking in:

- **Poll** — binary choice relevant to the niche or a decision the creator
  is actually making ("which thumbnail?" "A or B recipe?")
- **Quiz** — tests audience knowledge in a fun, low-stakes way; good for
  educational niches
- **Question box** — invites the audience to ask anything; strong for
  community-building and surfaces future content ideas
- **Behind the scenes (BTS)** — process, setup, mistakes, unfiltered
  moments; humanizes the creator and builds parasocial trust
- **Countdown** — for launches, events, or "X days until..." builds
  anticipation for something specific, not vague hype
- **AMA (Ask Me Anything)** — periodic, not daily; works best when
  announced ahead of time so the audience can prepare questions
- **Daily life / day-in-the-life** — casual, unscripted; good for
  personal branding and making the creator feel accessible
- **Reaction/duet-style response** — reacting to a trend, comment, or
  DM (with permission) — cheap to produce, high relatability

## Daily rhythm suggestion

A reasonable default rhythm (adjust to the creator's actual audience
activity times, not a fixed template):

- **Morning**: something light — a question, a poll, or "what I'm working
  on today" — sets context for the day
- **Midday/Afternoon**: BTS or process content — natural since it's when
  most creators are actually mid-task
- **Evening**: reflective or interactive — AMA-style, question box, or
  a recap poll ("which post did you like more today?")

This is a starting rhythm, not a rule — the point is variety and honest
timing around when the creator is actually doing something worth sharing.

## What makes a Story worth watching to completion

- Specific, not vague ("Ask me anything about editing" beats "Ask me
  anything")
- Visually distinct from the last Story, so it doesn't feel repetitive
  in the queue
- Genuinely interactive elements (poll/quiz/question stickers) outperform
  passive ones for reach, since interaction is a strong ranking signal

## Guardrail

Countdown/urgency formats must reflect something real (an actual launch
date, actual event) — never fabricated scarcity or fake urgency. Check
against `ethics/safety-guardrails.md`.
