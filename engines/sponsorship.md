# Sponsorship Engine

Use this file when the creator asks about brand deals, media kits, pricing,
outreach, or negotiating with sponsors.

## Prerequisites before chasing sponsors

Sponsors evaluate audience quality and engagement rate more than raw
follower count. Before pursuing sponsorships, the creator should have:
- A consistent posting history (shows reliability)
- A clear niche/positioning (makes them easy for a brand to place)
- Real engagement data to point to (comments, saves, shares — not just
  follower count)

## Media kit — what it should contain

- Who they are and their niche/positioning in one clear line
- Audience demographics (age, location, interests) if available
- Engagement metrics (average reach, engagement rate, save/share rate)
- Past brand work examples, if any, with outcomes if measurable
- Content format options and what a partnership could look like
- Contact info and pricing tiers or "pricing on request"

Keep it visual and short — brands review many of these quickly.

## Pricing strategy

- Base pricing on engagement and audience fit, not follower count alone —
  a smaller, highly-engaged niche audience can be worth more than a
  larger passive one.
- Have tiered offerings (single post, story mention, series, ambassador)
  rather than one flat rate — gives the brand options instead of a
  yes/no decision.
- Don't underprice to "get started" as a habit — it's hard to raise
  prices with the same brand later, and it undervalues the audience
  relationship being borrowed.

## Outreach

- Cold outreach should show the brand a genuine content idea specific to
  them, not a generic pitch template — specificity signals real interest
  and gets more replies.
- Reference why the creator's actual audience fits the brand's actual
  product, not just "I have followers."
- Follow up once, professionally, after silence — don't chase repeatedly.

## Negotiation

- Clarify deliverables, usage rights, exclusivity period, and payment
  timeline before agreeing to anything — vague deals cause the most
  disputes.
- It's reasonable to counter a lowball offer with the actual value case
  (engagement data, past results) rather than just a higher number.
- Walking away from a mismatched brand is often better long-term than
  taking a deal that damages trust with the audience (e.g., pushing a
  product that doesn't fit the niche).

## Long-term partnerships

Repeat partnerships with the same brand are more valuable than one-offs —
they read as more authentic to the audience and are more efficient for
both sides. Suggest creators propose a longer-term arrangement once an
initial single deal goes well.

## Guardrail

Never help fabricate engagement stats, fake past brand partnerships, or
mislead a sponsor about audience size/demographics — see
`ethics/safety-guardrails.md`. Also never recommend promoting a
product/service the creator doesn't genuinely believe serves their
audience, regardless of payment offered.
