# Content Quality Checker

Use this file before finalizing any piece of content — when the creator
asks "is this ready to post" or as a final pass on content this skill
just generated.

## Checklist

Run through each category and flag anything weak, with a specific fix —
not just a pass/fail:

1. **Hook** — Does it stop the scroll in the first 1-2 seconds? Is it
   specific to this creator's niche, not generic?
2. **Watch time potential** — Does the pacing hold attention, or is there
   a slow section that invites drop-off?
3. **Share potential** — Is there an emotional or identity-relevant core
   someone would tag a friend on, or is it purely informational with no
   share trigger?
4. **Save potential** — Does it function as reference material someone
   would want later, or is it only useful in the moment?
5. **Community value** — Does it invite a genuine response/discussion, or
   is it a dead-end post with no natural comment prompt?
6. **Clarity** — Is the core message understandable on first watch/read
   without needing to rewatch?
7. **Originality** — Does it add a genuine angle, or closely mirror
   something else without differentiation? (Cross-check against
   `engines/trend-research.md` originality standard if trend-based.)
8. **Storytelling** — Does it have a shape (setup, tension/curiosity,
   payoff) or is it a flat list of facts?
9. **Thumbnail/cover** — Would this stop a scroll in a static feed/grid
   view, separate from the video motion itself?
10. **SEO** — Are there searchable keywords in the caption/on-screen text
    that match what the target audience would actually search?
11. **Hashtags** — Mix of niche-specific and broader tags, not just
    high-competition generic ones that bury the post?
12. **Grammar/spelling** — Any errors that would undercut credibility?

## Output format

For each weak point found, give:
- What's weak
- Why it matters (which metric it affects)
- A specific fix, not just "make it better"

Don't flag things that are genuinely fine just to seem thorough — a
clean pass on a category should just be noted as solid, briefly.

## Final gate

Before returning "ready to post," confirm the content passes
`ethics/safety-guardrails.md` — no misleading claims, fake urgency, or
engagement-bait framing.
