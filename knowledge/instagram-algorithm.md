# Instagram Algorithm & Ranking Knowledge

Reference this file when advice depends on understanding how Instagram
actually surfaces content, not just what "feels" like good content.

## Important caveat

Instagram's ranking systems change over time and details aren't fully
public. Treat this file as general, durable principles rather than exact
current mechanics — and if the creator asks about a very specific recent
algorithm change, note that this should be verified against current
information rather than assumed static.

## Core principle across all surfaces

Instagram ranks content by predicting how likely a specific viewer is to
engage with it meaningfully — not by follower count or posting frequency
alone. Different surfaces (Feed, Stories, Reels, Explore, Search, Audio
pages) predict different things.

## Feed

Prioritizes signals that predict a viewer will engage: likely to comment,
likely to spend time on the post, likely to share it, relationship
strength between viewer and creator (past interactions matter). Recency
matters but is secondary to predicted interest.

## Stories

Ranked heavily by relationship signals — how often the viewer interacts
with this creator's content, DMs exchanged, past Story views/replies.
This is why Stories reward consistent daily presence more than viral
spikes; they're a relationship-strength surface, not a discovery surface.

## Reels

Optimizes for entertainment/discovery — prioritizes completion rate,
rewatches, and shares heavily, since these signal strong content
regardless of whether the viewer follows the creator. This is the primary
surface for reaching non-followers, which is why hook strength matters
disproportionately here (a weak hook loses the discovery opportunity in
the first second or two).

## Explore

Similar discovery logic to Reels but spans multiple content formats;
weighted toward content that keeps a viewer browsing (session-level
engagement), not just single-post engagement.

## Search

Increasingly keyword/caption-driven — meaning captions and on-screen text
with genuinely relevant, specific keywords (not keyword-stuffing) help
content surface to people actively searching, which is a different intent
than passive scrolling and often converts to saves/follows at a higher
rate.

## Audio pages

Using trending audio can add discoverability through the audio's own page,
but this is a minor boost relative to the content's own completion/
engagement performance — audio choice supports good content, it doesn't
replace it.

## Practical implications for this skill's advice

- Hook strength matters most for Reels/Explore (cold discovery surfaces)
- Consistency matters most for Stories (relationship-strength surface)
- Specific, searchable language in captions matters for Search surface
- Completion rate and rewatches are disproportionately valuable signals —
  this is why the Script Writing Engine emphasizes pacing and payoff
