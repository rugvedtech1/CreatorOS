# Copywriting Principles for Captions & Hooks

Reference this file whenever writing captions, hooks, or any on-screen/
spoken text — used alongside `engines/script-writing.md`.

## Captions serve a different job than the video/photo

The visual stops the scroll; the caption either deepens engagement (adds
context, invites a response) or kills momentum (repeats what's already
visible, or reads as an afterthought). Never treat the caption as a
formality — it's a second chance to earn a comment, save, or follow.

## Opening line discipline

Instagram truncates captions after roughly one to two lines before
"more" — meaning the first line functions like a second hook. Never
front-load a caption with a generic greeting or setup; lead with the most
interesting or specific line, same discipline as a video hook.

## Specificity over cleverness

A specific, concrete line ("I shot this after three failed attempts in
the rain") builds more credibility and interest than a clever but vague
one ("chasing the perfect shot"). When in doubt, choose the version with
more concrete detail.

## Caption structures that work

- **Story format** — brief narrative that adds context the visual alone
  doesn't show (what happened before/after, why this moment mattered)
- **Question format** — a genuine question tied to the content, inviting
  real responses rather than generic "thoughts?"
- **List/value format** — for educational content, a tight list format in
  the caption reinforces save-worthiness (people save it to reference the
  list later)
- **Single strong statement** — sometimes the most powerful caption is one
  sharp line with no elaboration; don't pad for length if it weakens the
  line

## CTA phrasing

Match the phrasing to the actual goal (see `engines/script-writing.md`
CTA guidance) — generic CTAs ("like and follow!") underperform because
they don't tell the viewer *why* this specific action matters right now.

## Hashtag copywriting note

Hashtags function as searchable keywords, not decoration — choose based
on what the target audience would actually type into search, mixing
niche-specific and broader relevant tags rather than defaulting to the
highest-volume generic tags, which bury a post among enormous competition.

## What to avoid

- Keyword-stuffing captions in ways that read unnaturally to a human
  reader (hurts trust even if intended for search)
- Caption length mismatched to platform norm for the content type (a
  one-word caption on a piece that needed context, or an essay under a
  simple visual that didn't need one)
- Manipulative curiosity gaps that don't pay off in the caption or video
  (e.g., "you won't believe what happened" with no actual payoff) — this
  damages trust and is covered under `ethics/safety-guardrails.md`
